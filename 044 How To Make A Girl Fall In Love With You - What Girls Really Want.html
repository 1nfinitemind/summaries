How To Make A Girl Fall In Love With You - What Girls Really Want

  • Leo's personal journey: Leo went from having poor dating skills to engaging in years of study and real–world practice in the pickup community to understand attraction, female psychology, and how to build relationships with women.
  • Attraction versus Love: He emphasizes that attraction is the precursor to love, and if a person can create attraction, love often follows. Removing barriers is key to allowing love to develop naturally.
  • Personality over looks: Women are more attracted to personality and the emotions a man can make them feel, not just his physical appearance. Leo dispels the misconception that looks are the key factor for men in attracting women.
  • Emotional range is attractive: Women are attracted to men who can offer a range of emotional experiences, not just positive ones. They appreciate men who are humorous, independent, and capable of surprising them.
  • Metaphor of the ship and passenger: Leo uses this metaphor to describe how women seek men who can lead them on exciting life journeys, akin to a ship taking a passenger to various exciting destinations.
  • Emotional stimulation on micro and macro levels: Attraction starts with making women feel unique emotions on a small scale in initial interactions and extends to continually providing engaging experiences in a long–term relationship.
  • The importance of sex in a relationship: Leo stresses that women enjoy sex and that men should focus on their partner's pleasure and be experimental in bed to keep the relationship strong.
  • Maintaining purpose and direction: Men should not become too needy or make their partners the center of their world; having a strong purpose and independence is attractive to women.
  • Avoiding emasculation in relationships: Men should avoid becoming too accommodating or submissive, as women prefer a partner who is decisive, has direction, and can take the lead.
  • Constant self–improvement: Staying attractive requires men to continually work on themselves, develop emotional self–mastery, and strive for personal success to be a catch for their partners.
