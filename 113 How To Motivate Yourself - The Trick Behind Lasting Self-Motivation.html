How To Motivate Yourself - The Trick Behind Lasting Self-Motivation

  • Self–Motivation Mastery: Self–motivation is about generating drive from within, rather than relying on external factors like motivational videos, bosses, or other influences. It requires self–reliance and internal drive.
  • Two Levels of Motivational Challenges: Leo distinguishes between motivation for day–to–day tasks, like going to the gym or completing a report, and a deeper, more profound lack of motivation which is related to lack of direction in life–a feeling that your life lacks passion, energy, and is not progressing.
  • Signs of a Misaligned Life: Struggling to find motivation in daily tasks could be an indicator that your life is out of alignment with your true passions. The example given is of a person passionate about music but working a 9–to–5 job that feels unfulfilling–the real issue is the need to realign life's priorities with this passion.
  • Authentic Self vs. Conditioned Self: There's often a disconnection between one's authentic desires and their current life structure, dominated by conditioned routines and societal expectations. Fulfillment comes from aligning life with the authentic self and pursuing passions intensely, rather than fixing superficial problems.
  • The Importance of a Vivid Goal Vision: To truly motivate oneself, it's essential to have a clear, specific, and compelling vision or picture of what you want. Whether it's fitness, nutrition, or personal relationships, this goal vision has to be vivid, detailed, and ambitious.
  • Creating a Motivational Vision: Writing out or visually sketching the details of one's goal can significantly enhance motivation. This exercise cements a tangible and attractive vision to strive toward.
  • Contextualizing Your Vision: Integrating the vision into the larger context of one's life and future is crucial. This process involves imagining the broader impacts of achieving the vision and how it would ripple through the entirety of one's existence over time.
  • Long–term Visualization: Envision your future by considering what your life would look like years from now if you achieve your goals, including positive impacts on health, work, relationships, and energy levels.
  • Consequences of Inaction: Also, vividly imagine the negative outcomes if you do not pursue your goal, such as deteriorating health, poor work performance, strained relationships, and potential severe illnesses in the distant future.
  • Implementation of Habitual Visualization: Recognize that a one–time visual exercise is insufficient and commit to reconnecting with your vision frequently using techniques like journaling, visualizations, affirmations, and meditation.
  • Daily Reconnection with Vision: Leo stresses the importance of choosing a daily habit that helps reconnect with your vision, whether it's reviewing a vision document, visualizing during a routine activity like showering, or discussing your goals with a supportive friend or partner.
  • Personal Example of Maintaining Motivation: Leo shares his own tactic to stay motivated while bodybuilding; he flexed in the mirror before and after gym sessions to observe muscle growth, providing immediate visual feedback and ongoing motivation.
  • Reflecting on Accomplishments: Regularly take time to appreciate the work accomplished towards your goals, as it helps maintain motivation, reconnect with your vision, and prompts consideration of the next steps.
  • Engagement and Further Learning: Leo concludes by encouraging the sharing of content to spread value and offers further educational resources including a newsletter subscription, a video series on limiting beliefs, and a chance to win personal coaching sessions.
