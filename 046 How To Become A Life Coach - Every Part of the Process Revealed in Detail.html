How To Become A Life Coach - Every Part of the Process Revealed in Detail

  • Credibility of coaching programs : It's essential to join a life coach training program that is reputable, well-reviewed, and offers certification through the International Coaching Federation (ICF). The ICF is a global standard body for coaching, so aligning with their accredited schools is advisable.
  • Benefits of certification programs : Although not strictly necessary, certification programs teach the fundamental aspects of being a coach. They offer practical experience and critical techniques that books or seminars alone cannot provide.
  • Cost of quality coaching programs : A substantial coaching program may cost between eight to ten thousand dollars, like the one provided by Ipek. This investment is competitive and reasonable compared to other professional trainings and educations.
  • Structure of the Ipek program : The program takes about ten months and is broken into four modules, each followed by a few months for homework and coaching practice. This schedule allows one to train while still maintaining their current job.
  • Practical experience in coaching : In-person and over-the-phone coaching training is provided in a low-pressure environment among peers within the program, accompanied by immediate feedback from instructors.
  • Going beyond the program : To excel as a coach, one should seek additional practice outside the program assignments by partnering with other trainees or seeking client opportunities, aiming for around 100 practice calls to gain confidence.
  • Setting up a coaching business : Besides coaching skills, a coach needs to understand business, marketing, sales, and how to source clients. A good coaching program should provide business and marketing support to help set up a sustainable practice.
  • Specializing is key : Selecting a niche such as life purpose, spirituality, career, money, fitness, or even a sub-specialty within these areas is crucial for marketability and success.
  • Entrepreneurial mindset : Coaches need to operate with an entrepreneurial mindset, as most will be self-employed, requiring a strong understanding of business, goal-setting, and self-motivation.
  • Living as an example : It is vital for a coach to practice personal development actively and be the embodiment of the lifestyle they are selling to gain clients' trust.
